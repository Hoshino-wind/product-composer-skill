# Chinese Aesthetic

## Principle

Translate a supported Chinese cultural source through spatial rhythm, restraint, material, and modern product clarity before using symbols. This optional Modifier consumes the active `DirectionContract` and does not create a secondary route hierarchy; the selected product or brand route continues to own user mission, information priority, proof, and conversion.

This module is operational design guidance, not a claim to canonical art history. Cultural specificity requires provenance; beauty never authorizes an invented fact.

## Source contract

Name the Cultural source as a craft, material, place, object, venue, product story, or documented visual system. Record source, access status, relevant traits, authorization, and uncertainty in the active Design Contract.

For a broad source-inferred brief, retain the selected Surface and Cultural Modifier; follow the SKILL.md sequential-window rule rather than substituting Brand for Product or Hybrid. Record cultural source status in this owner. When missing media constrains truth, proof, accessibility, or composition, close the current window and hand off to Asset truth even when no file exists; resume from the updated Design Contract. Open the reference owner only for an actual supplied reference. Verify remains a subsequent stage.

User-provided material may establish the requested direction or content, but it does not by itself verify a named cultural claim. A named cultural claim requires a trustworthy source and enough context to identify what the source supports; without both, keep the claim unresolved or remove its factual specificity.

If the brief asks only for a broad modern Chinese aesthetic and supplies no specific source, mark it `source-inferred`. A source-inferred direction may use the structural grammar below and broad material families, but it must not invent a named dynasty, artifact, region, museum collection, poem, calligraphy, religion, maker, or historical attribution.

When any named cultural claim affects the experience, verify it against that trustworthy source and context even when the user supplied the material or requested the claim. User intent and asset authorization do not establish historical, institutional, geographic, religious, or artifact truth.

## Design grammar

Use these as mechanisms with observable consequences:

- `留白`: reserve a quiet field that protects the primary object, action, or line of thought.
- `气韵`: carry visual momentum through hierarchy, scroll rhythm, repeated edge, and changing density.
- `虚实`: balance inspectable product proof with softer atmosphere without confusing their truth roles.
- `藏露`: reveal the detail or capability needed now while allowing depth to emerge through interaction or continuation.
- `借景`: frame a product object through an aperture, shelf, shadow, fold, window, screen edge, or environmental relationship.
- `层次`: establish clear foreground, middle ground, and background rather than flat motif wallpaper.
- `器物感`: let material, join, weight, edge, light, and tactility make the primary object feel considered.
- `移步换景`: let each viewport change the view and narrative job while remaining in one spatial world.

Select only the mechanisms that serve the user mission and source. Naming the grammar without changing composition does not satisfy it.

## Modern product translation

Preserve task correctness, interaction clarity, state, permissions, accessibility, and information architecture. Chinese aesthetic may shape rhythm, material, framing, hierarchy, and transitions; it must not hide filters, tables, actions, errors, or dense evidence.

- Let a real work object carry the beauty: review desk, collection shelf, proof ledger, comparison surface, approval sheet, or another job-native object.
- Use material roles subtly in repeated operational UI; reserve stronger expression for one dominant object or transition.
- Give seal-like marks only a real commitment or verification meaning. Do not use them as decorative status chips.
- Keep ordinary product language. Do not rename normal software with grand pseudo-industrial or invented historical labels.
- Ensure the static, mobile, loading, empty, error, disabled, and focus states remain understandable without cultural decoration.

## Brand translation

Build one coherent world from the source rather than attaching motifs to a generic landing page:

- Choose one dominant scene and one structural framing motif.
- Establish a movement motif that connects the first viewport to later proof, story, or conversion.
- Use one detail motif and one UI motif at subordinate scale.
- Make product, offer, venue, artifact, or portfolio proof inspectable.
- Keep navigation and action visually assimilated but immediately usable.
- Let `移步换景` change the view across sections while palette, material, type roles, and edge behavior preserve continuity.

Generated atmosphere may support the world but cannot stand in for an official artifact, real venue, documented object, or claimed outcome.

## Palette and type

Derive palette roles from the Cultural source and material evidence, not from the category label `Chinese`. Possible material families include ink and paper, porcelain and celadon, lacquer and bronze, stone and mineral pigment, silk and bamboo, or clean native software with one sourced accent.

Assign base, surface, text, quiet text, border, semantic, emotional-accent, and action roles. Use one rare accent; do not combine many auspicious colors by habit. Red and gold require evidence such as ceremony, festival, material, or an established brand system.

Keep body and control type highly readable. Decorative or calligraphic type belongs only to a sourced display asset or declared atmosphere. Never fabricate calligraphy as factual writing, and never make a decorative face carry actions, values, or long copy.

## Anti-stereotype rules

Reject decorative shorthand unless the verified subject specifically requires it:

- universal red-gold treatment
- dragons, lanterns, pagodas, fans, coins, knots, clouds, seals, or brush strokes used as stickers
- tourist-poster collages of mountains, temples, artifacts, and calligraphy
- mixed dynasty, region, religion, and craft signals with no source relationship
- fake poems, inscriptions, catalog numbers, artifact labels, provenance, or museum language
- antique interfaces that obstruct modern tasks
- a generic Western luxury layout with Chinese symbols added afterward

Motifs must arise from the source, perform a structural or semantic job, and remain subordinate to proof and usability.

## Repair

When the result feels generic, cheap, or theatrical:

1. Remove literal symbols and unsupported factual cues.
2. Reconfirm the source contract and downgrade unsupported specificity to source-inferred.
3. Rebuild around one dominant scene and one structural grammar mechanism.
4. Increase silence, proportion, framing, and layered depth before adding detail.
5. Replace category colors with a material-derived palette and explicit roles.
6. Restore inspectable product proof, task clarity, and usable actions.
7. Check that the world survives without cultural labels; if not, the structure is still generic.

## Acceptance checks

- Cultural source and access status are recorded; unsupported specificity is source-inferred.
- Selected grammar mechanisms have visible spatial or interaction consequences.
- The Modifier delta preserves the active route and states any contract overrides.
- Modern product tasks, states, controls, and accessibility remain clear.
- Brand sections continue one spatial world instead of restarting as card grids.
- Palette and type derive from material or verified brand evidence.
- Generated atmosphere does not impersonate cultural or factual proof.
- User-provided material does not verify a named cultural claim without a trustworthy source and sufficient context.
- Generated-media provenance never converts depicted cultural content into factual or authority proof.
- Stereotypes, mixed-source collage, fake writing, and fake provenance are absent.
