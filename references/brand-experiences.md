# Brand Experiences

## Principle

Build a complete, truthful experience architecture rather than a stack of fashionable screens. Let website archetype, real content, media, and interaction determine the opener, page rhythm, proof, exploration, and commitment path. Atmosphere, product proof, editorial depth, and conversion may lead at different moments; none is a universal first-section recipe.

## Content-Truth Questions

Answer the Content-truth questions before choosing an experience architecture:

- Introduced: What or who is being introduced, in concrete category language?
- Audience: Who should recognize that this is for them?
- Core content object: Is the experience organized around a product UI, physical object, service process, body of work, place, archive, collection, or another real subject?
- Proof: What artifact, workflow, result, credential, catalog record, or sourced fact supports the promise?
- Trust: Why should this audience believe the source and the claim?
- Next action or continuation: Should the viewer try, buy, book, contact, compare, explore, inspect, or simply continue a cultural or editorial journey?

Mark supplied facts, interpretations, and placeholders separately. Do not invent metrics, testimonials, clients, links, awards, project outcomes, catalog items, or product states; use explicit placeholders.

For a Hybrid launch with no supplied media, keep the selected Hybrid Surface. When the absence materially constrains proof or composition, close the current window and hand off to Asset truth even when no file exists; resume from the updated Design Contract without replacing Product or Brand ownership.

## Reference-First Decision

When the user supplies a visual reference, follow it before offering alternatives. Extract its hierarchy, dominant silhouette, spatial relationships, type behavior, palette roles, material, proof placement, media ecology, and temporal logic; translate those relationships to the real content and constraints without copying incidental decoration.

When the source is a gallery, award platform, mood-board feed, or case library, study the works rather than the platform shell. Classify each actual case by archetype and inspect the complete route from opener to footer or interaction endpoint. A gallery tile, looping preview, or first-viewport screenshot can prove only what it visibly contains; it cannot prove a runtime scroll or interaction mechanism.

Record a full-page mechanism trace per case:

```text
Case trace
- case archetype and user job:
- route or page map:
- ordered content beats:
- region geometry and scroll length:
- media set and reuse:
- trigger, state change, and input verb:
- handoff into the next region or mode:
- proof, trust, conversion, or orientation placement:
- transferable mechanism:
- conditional boundary and non-transferable skin:
- evidence level: live runtime | recording | full-page still | first viewport
```

Promote recurring relationships into general guidance only when they survive comparison across different subjects. Also preserve a rare, well-observed mechanism in a conditional library when its enabling content and constraints are explicit. Popularity is not evidence of universal fit.

Offer `2–3` previews only when direction is genuinely unresolved. If reference and content already determine the direction, state the interpretation and proceed with one contract. When a reference conflicts with verified content, accessibility, platform behavior, or available media, name the conflict and preserve the closest compatible relationship.

## Website Archetype And Experience Scope

Classify the target before borrowing any case mechanism:

| Website archetype | Primary job | Typical evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| brand or campaign | establish identity, world, meaning, or launch memory | real product, people, place, material, history, film, or authored imagery |
| landing or conversion | move one audience from promise to an earned action | mechanism, outcome, objection handling, trust, and offer |
| SaaS or product-led | explain a product through real states and workflows | live DOM UI, screenshots, recordings, data states, code, integrations, and customer results |
| service or commerce | make an offer, process, specification, and transaction understandable | service process, product details, materials, availability, price, and purchase state |
| portfolio or editorial | make work, authorship, or a body of knowledge inspectable | projects, artwork, writing, metadata, details, and alternative collection views |
| experimental or cultural | let time, place, archive, or spatial rules become the experience | sourced events, maps, timelines, panoramas, sound, artifacts, and orientation cues |
| hybrid | combine two or more of the above without losing either job | explicit transition between their content, proof, and action models |

Separate these scopes:

- `opener or hero`: the opening experience within a route; it may contain several beats, regions, media, and states and may span multiple viewports
- `route or page experience`: the complete journey from entry through deeper content to its footer, terminal action, or bounded exploratory mode
- `multi-route website`: the route architecture, shared navigation, detail pages, product pages, collections, legal/trust surfaces, and cross-route states required for a complete brand presence

Hero does not mean one `100vh` section, one image, or the whole website. It also does not imply a slider, a fixed screen count, or full-screen geometry. A complete website may combine a multi-state opener with ordinary content flow, short editorial bands, pinned chapters, product walkthroughs, horizontal collections, FAQs, detail routes, and a conventional footer.

Record `target device scope: PC-only | responsive | named devices`. An explicit PC-only request removes mobile layout work; it does not remove keyboard, focus, reduced-motion, loading, interruption, or pointer/wheel usability.

## Content And Region Model

Compose from independent axes rather than equating content with screens:

- `content beat`: one meaningful introduction, argument, proof, transformation, collection item, objection, action, or emotional change
- `region`: the layout and interaction container that carries one or more beats
- `viewport`: the currently visible window, never an automatic section boundary
- `state`: a meaningful condition within a region before, during, or after input
- `media set`: zero, one, or many media objects assigned by communication role

A beat may be shorter than, equal to, or longer than a viewport. One region may contain several scroll-driven states; several beats may share a region; one long-form beat may cross several visual subregions. Do not force every region into a full viewport and do not create a region merely to satisfy a page-count target.

Record the experience before composing individual layouts:

```text
Experience architecture
- website archetype and route/page scope:
- opener scope and first read:
- ordered content beats:
- proof, trust, conversion, and orientation rhythm:
- continuity and signature system:
- target device scope and input model:

Region record
- region job and content beats served:
- height mode: intrinsic | subviewport | viewport | overviewport/pinned | horizontal track | spatial canvas
- pin range, internal travel, or thresholds:
- states and state progression:
- media set:
- entry, handoff, and exit:
- action, proof, or orientation role:
- loading, interruption, reduced-motion, and static behavior:
```

The sequence should be causal when the content is causal, but not every portfolio, collection, or cultural exploration needs a linear argument. If regions can be reordered, determine whether that expresses legitimate collection freedom or an accidental lack of structure.

## First-Viewport Contract

The first viewport is an entry sample of the larger experience, not the definition of the hero or site. Design it after archetype, route scope, content beats, and region geometry are known.

Require a clear first read, a credible reason to continue, stable navigation or orientation, and immediate access to any essential action. Do not universally require one screenshot, one proof object, one CTA, or at most three content zones:

- SaaS and conversion pages usually need early category clarity, product or mechanism evidence, and an action path.
- Brand and campaign pages may lead with a content-relevant person, object, place, film, type field, or atmosphere when deeper regions deliver the required proof and offer.
- Portfolios may let the work itself dominate and defer commercial actions to details or contact.
- Experimental and cultural experiences may lead with an orientation or entry verb rather than a sales CTA.

Reject an entry that is visually complete but leaves no legible path into the rest of the route. Also reject a first viewport that tries to summarize every later beat.

## Media Choreography Gate

For a media-led Brand or Hybrid experience, use this order before visual implementation:

1. `website archetype and route scope`: define the complete target and opener boundary.
2. `content beats`: establish what the experience introduces, develops, proves, explores, and resolves.
3. `region geometry and scroll model`: choose intrinsic, viewport, pinned, horizontal, spatial, or mixed flow because the content needs it.
4. `media palette and many-to-many mapping`: map communication roles, truth roles, and reuse without allocating one asset per region.
5. `motion grammar and per-region choreography`: define triggers, state progression, handoffs, and input control.
6. `typographic voice and interaction`: preserve semantic text and native controls within the art direction.
7. `implementation`: compose the regions, media, transitions, and states for the declared target devices.

One content beat may use no external media or several synchronized media. One asset may persist through several beats. One region may combine stills, image sequences, video, screen recordings, live DOM UI, SVG, canvas/WebGL/3D, shaders, typographic fields, textures, or audio. Resource sufficiency is judged by communication, truth, and state coverage, not by screen count or independent-source count.

```text
Media choreography record
- media-world thesis:
- source id and truth role:
- content beats and regions served:
- communication role:
- state progression served:
- relationship to other media:
- reuse, derivations, and continuity:
- trigger and handoff:
- target-device treatment:
- poster, loading, failure, reduced-motion, and static fallback:
```

Derivatives remain one source for provenance, but provenance count is not an aesthetic score. Reuse may be the strongest continuity decision when the same object legitimately changes state, as in a product walkthrough or spatial system. Essential wording must remain selectable semantic text or have an equivalent accessible label. Informative video or audio needs the appropriate captions, transcript, or equivalent.

When an unresolved direction needs previews, represent the full proposed architecture: show ordinary-flow regions as well as immersive ones, the media reuse graph, state changes, and handoffs. Do not use a row of equally sized hero posters as a substitute for a complete route.

## Product Proof And Belief Timing

Place real proof according to archetype and commitment risk rather than applying `proof-before-vibes` to every website.

- SaaS and product-led: show inspectable product UI, workflow, data state, code, integration, or output early enough to substantiate the promise.
- Landing and service: connect claim to mechanism, outcome, risk, objection handling, and a credible next step.
- Commerce: make product, material, specification, availability, price, and purchase state inspectable before commitment.
- Brand and campaign: atmosphere may lead when it is made from real subject matter; the route must later reveal concrete identity, product, history, place, people, or work.
- Portfolio and editorial: the authored work and its metadata may be the proof.
- Experimental and cultural: source precision, orientation, events, artifacts, and access to primary media may replace commercial proof.

Generated atmosphere never substitutes for authentic product, person, place, catalog, historical, or outcome evidence. If real proof is unavailable, label a concept or placeholder and narrow the claim.

## Page Rhythm And Continuation

Mix region geometries according to reading and interaction needs. Ordinary document flow is often the best container for explanation, FAQ, pricing, specifications, testimonials, or long-form editorial content. Use pinned, horizontal, full-viewport, or spatial regions only when their state model earns the interruption.

Vary density, media ratio, color field, and motion energy over the route. Continuity may come from a persistent object, route line, crop, horizon, material seam, type posture, navigation behavior, audio field, or transformation rule; it does not require every region to share one layout or one effect.

Give each handoff a job: deepen, prove, compare, release, reorient, or invite action. Pinned regions need a clear entry and exit. Horizontal tracks need progress and an obvious return to vertical flow. Spatial canvases need orientation, escape, and an accessible path to their content.

## Desire Mechanisms

Use desire mechanisms where emotional pull helps the real content:

- curiosity: reveal an understandable gap without withholding essential meaning
- aspiration: show a credible better state of the audience's work or identity
- relief: make friction visibly disappear
- confidence: make precision, proof, and control feel attainable
- mastery: show an excellent result the viewer can plausibly create
- participation: expose a real judgment, configuration, exploration, or refinement loop
- belonging: express a sourced world or standard the audience wants to join
- progress: make the next useful step feel close and low-risk

A region may combine mechanisms when hierarchy remains clear; another region may need none. Do not assign one mechanism mechanically to every viewport. Avoid fake `drag`, `click`, `scroll`, or `swipe` labels when the interface does not provide that behavior.

## Signature System

Build a signature system from content-native sources:

1. Core content object: product, people, place, work, material, process, archive, or collection.
2. Dominant spatial or temporal thesis: stage, route, map, instrument, publication, catalog, transformation, or another fitting model.
3. Motif hierarchy: framing, movement, detail, and interface motifs with named jobs.
4. Source-derived palette and material behavior.
5. Type roles for brand voice, reading, metadata, control, and data.
6. Motion grammar: shared timing, transformation, input, and handoff principles, not one repeated preset.
7. Media ecology: a coherent many-to-many set rather than interchangeable decoration.
8. Attention control: simultaneous hierarchy and sequential pacing, including where the experience deliberately becomes rich.

A system fails when changing the logo would make it fit any brand, imagery is a sticker layer, every region starts a new unrelated world, or continuity is faked by repeating the same entrance animation.

## Conversion Path

For commercial experiences, connect earned understanding to an action such as preview, compare, try, start, book, buy, contact, subscribe, or request. Keep action language consistent with the promise and proof; explain cost, risk, prerequisites, or reversibility before high-commitment actions.

Not every experience has a commercial conversion. Portfolios may resolve in project detail, inquiry, or contact. Cultural and experimental work may resolve in exploration completion, archive access, sharing, education, or no explicit CTA. Record the intended terminal state instead of forcing a marketing button.

## Market Calibration Discipline

Calibrate within the selected archetype, audience, content maturity, and interaction cost:

| Archetype | Common strong architecture | Common failure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| brand or campaign | content-native world, authored media, editorial development, product/history/people proof | empty cinematic mood, generic luxury skin, no deeper identity |
| landing or conversion | promise, mechanism, proof, objection handling, commitment | repeating feature cards, unsupported prestige, CTA before understanding |
| SaaS or product-led | real UI and workflow walkthrough, integrations, install/use evidence, results, trust | abstract orb, dashboard collage, animations unrelated to product state |
| service or commerce | offer, process, specification, trust, availability, transaction | desire without details, details without product presence, hidden risk |
| portfolio or editorial | work-first collection, alternative views, metadata, detail routes | generic agency shell, full-screen treatment for weak assets, inaccessible browsing |
| experimental or cultural | spatial/time model, orientation, sourced nodes, multimodal exploration | spectacle without content, trapped navigation, unlabeled archive media |

Every region may use a distinct effect when the effect is earned by its content and the sequence shares an intentional motion grammar. Judge simultaneous competition, redundancy, handoff quality, runtime cost, and input control—not the raw count of effects. Repeating one preset everywhere and forcing one global effect are both failures.

Use full-viewport cinematic luxury only when a credible dominant subject and brand tempo support it. Continue into ordinary-height detail, service, history, specification, or conversion regions as needed. Black backgrounds, serif type, glow, glass, and slow parallax are not a reusable luxury formula.

## Acceptance Checks

- Is the target classified as a brand, landing, SaaS, service/commerce, portfolio/editorial, experimental/cultural, or explicit hybrid website?
- Are opener, complete route, and multi-route scope separated instead of treating hero as the whole site?
- Does the content/region model keep beats, regions, viewports, states, media, and effects independent?
- Are intrinsic, subviewport, viewport, pinned, horizontal, spatial, and ordinary-flow regions chosen by content rather than a screen recipe?
- Does a reference study inspect the complete route or clearly label the evidence limit?
- Does every interactive region record trigger, state change, handoff, input control, and fallback?
- Does the media map allow zero-to-many media per beat and reuse one asset across several beats without tying source count to quality?
- Is proof timing calibrated to the archetype and commitment risk rather than one universal rule?
- Can different regions use different effects while remaining coherent through a named motion grammar?
- Does the declared target-device scope govern verification, including PC-only when explicitly requested?
- Are unsupported facts, product states, identities, results, and cultural claims labeled or omitted?
- Does the complete website remain usable, orienting, and truthful after the opener ends?
