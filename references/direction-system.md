# Direction System

## Principle

Choose an evidence-backed visual direction before composing an experience. Make the product job feel inevitable through one coherent spatial and temporal thesis, then use explicit constraints to keep taste from collapsing into decoration or a familiar template.

## Design Read

Design read: A [surface] for [audience] to [user job], expressed through [visual language], within [hard constraints].

## Aesthetic Derivation

Derive the direction from source signals instead of selecting a fashionable label. Start with the user mission, audience, core content object, available proof, task frequency and environment, supplied references or brand assets, platform constraints, and intended feeling. Separate observed facts, explicit user choices, and creative hypotheses.

For every material decision, preserve this causal trace:

```text
Aesthetic derivation
- source signal:
- inferred meaning:
- visible consequence:
- affected DirectionContract field:
- alternative rejected and why:
```

Build the direction in this order:

1. Name the meaning that should be understood or felt and the real proof that deserves attention.
2. Define the attention strategy: first focal point, second read, sequential reveal, and deliberate silence.
3. Choose a spatial relationship and dominant silhouette that make the mission legible at full size and thumbnail scale.
4. Define a visual genre as a plausible world of form, type, image, material, light, and time; do not use an artist, studio, or work name as the reasoning.
5. Create character through one tension pair, then state which force dominates and what remains restrained.
6. Translate the same causes into type roles, palette roles, material behavior, image-world logic, and motion or continuity.
7. Record fixed anchors for truth, identity, the local system, and the signature; name one exploration axis that may change in the next candidate batch.
8. Apply the Deletion rule and confirm that the direction survives without decorative explanation.

Do not accept adjectives without visible consequences. `Precise`, for example, must alter alignment, tolerance, edge behavior, timing, or evidence density; `warm` must alter light, material, color temperature, voice, or pacing. When direction is unresolved, create `2–3` internally coherent alternatives that differ structurally, then compare them as artifacts. Within one selected direction or repair batch, change only the named exploration axis so causality remains inspectable.

## Direction Contract

Write one `DirectionContract` before image generation or implementation and keep it as the shared decision record:

```text
DirectionContract
Design read:
Visual thesis:
user mission:
aesthetic stance:
tension pair:
visual genre:
attention strategy:
Style family:
dominant silhouette:
composition family:
experience architecture:
page rhythm and continuity:
Composition variance: 1-5
Motion energy: 1-5
Information density: 1-5
material language:
image-world thesis: not applicable | [world and continuity logic]
palette roles:
type roles:
signature detail:
justified risk:
restraint:
fixed anchors:
exploration axis:
anti-defaults: [{pattern, task context, why weak, override evidence}]
Override condition:
Deletion rule:
```

Make the Visual thesis one decisive relationship among product job, form, time, and feeling. Describe the user mission as an outcome rather than a feature list. Write the aesthetic stance as three concrete qualities, the tension pair as two controlled forces with a named dominant force, the visual genre as a plausible world rather than a named imitation, and the attention strategy as an ordered allocation of focus. Treat the composition family as the spatial grammar and the dominant silhouette as the contour that should remain recognizable at thumbnail size.

Record fixed anchors that must not drift across UI, image, motion, or implementation. Record exactly one exploration axis for the next candidate or repair batch; change the axis only after evidence shows it is exhausted. Use `not applicable` for the image-world thesis when external or generated imagery is not material, so the contract never forces image generation. Name one justified risk and one restraint. Restraint controls attention, simultaneous competition, and pacing; it does not mean a low count of sections, media, or effects.

## Direction Parameters

Use a 1–5 anchored scale. The anchors are `1 stable/static/sparse`, `3 balanced/responsive/balanced`, and `5 expressive/choreographed/compressed`:

| Parameter | 1 | 3 | 5 | Infer from |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Composition variance | stable | balanced | expressive | How much structural departure the job, reference, and product maturity can support |
| Motion energy | static | responsive | choreographed | Whether time only confirms actions, explains continuity, or carries the experience |
| Information density | sparse | balanced | compressed | The amount of simultaneous evidence required to act correctly |

Use `2` and `4` only as positions between their adjacent anchors. Infer every value from user evidence, supplied references, real content, task frequency, and platform constraints. Define no global default. These parameters describe a selected direction; do not promote them into route axes or use them to choose the surface owner.

## Style Families

Select one Style family because its behavior fits the evidence, not because its label sounds attractive.

| Family | Evidence of fit | Direction consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Utility Product | Repeated work, records, forms, approvals, permissions, or recovery | Stable frame, meaningful work object, contextual control, disciplined density |
| Data Dense | Comparison, monitoring, analysis, or risk depends on many values at once | Compact hierarchy, explicit data grammar, semantic color, inspectable filters |
| Technical Instrument | Control, measurement, simulation, or spatial inspection is the product value | One precise control or inspection object with state attached to it |
| Editorial Premium | Authority, expertise, portfolio proof, or premium clarity leads | Art-directed type, asymmetric proof, controlled negative space |
| Cultural Immersive | A verified place, craft, collection, or cultural source carries the meaning | Source-native material, spatial continuity, restrained interface assimilation |
| Chinese Aesthetic | The brief explicitly calls for modern Chinese aesthetic structure | Source-led silence, framing, layered depth, material restraint, modern usability |
| Playful Consumer | Delight, habit, learning, or approachability matters more than dense control | Friendly form, controlled bright color, low-risk motion cues |
| Soft Craft | Creator work, writing, boutique product, or tactile authorship leads | Warm material, gentle contrast, hand-finished detail, real product proof |
| Bold Campaign | A launch, event, offer, or short conversion window needs urgency | High contrast, kinetic hierarchy, direct action path |

When a supplied reference or an established brand system is clear, follow that evidence first and use the nearest family only to state constraints. Domain rules still come from the surface owner; a family must not smuggle product, brand, or cultural behavior into the wrong route.

## Taste Contract

The Taste contract is the judgment slice of the `DirectionContract`, not a second brief. Populate it through:

- a three-word stance that makes the desired feeling concrete
- a tension pair that creates controlled contrast
- a signature form expressed by the dominant silhouette
- a signature restraint that states what stays quiet at the same moment and how richer states are paced over time
- an anti-reference translated into contextual anti-defaults
- one memorability hook expressed by the signature detail
- the existing Deletion rule that names what disappears first under pressure

Prefer fewer, stronger forms; proportion before ornament; and one specific detail over generic polish. Do not sit at the midpoint of every taste axis merely to appear safe.

## Shared Visual Grammar

Project one selected direction into UI and image decisions without making them visually identical:

| Direction decision | UI consequence | Image or asset consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| attention strategy | hierarchy, control placement, density, and state sequence | focal point, crop, negative space, and depth order |
| dominant silhouette | region contour, work-object scale, and grouping | subject or scene contour that survives thumbnail inspection |
| tension pair | type, scale, color, material, or motion contrast | camera, light, palette, material, or staging contrast |
| material language | surface, edge, depth, feedback, and component treatment | physical material, texture scale, reflection, atmosphere, and light response |
| page rhythm and continuity | spacing cadence, state progression, and handoff behavior | asset reuse, shot distance, crop sequence, and visual continuity |
| fixed anchors and exploration axis | protected system rules plus one inspectable variation | protected identity/world rules plus one controlled candidate delta |

Share causal rules, not a sticker layer. An image must not introduce a second palette, material world, light logic, or attention order that contradicts the UI unless the tension pair explicitly requires that contrast. A UI must not flatten an authored image into decoration when the image carries the selected proof, atmosphere, or transition role.

## Composition Families

Choose the spatial relationship that best exposes the product job:

- operational workbench or ledger for repeated record work
- split comparison for judgment between states or alternatives
- canvas or composer for direct assembly and manipulation
- timeline or sequence for causal, temporal, or approval flow
- atlas, map, matrix, or data wall for relational overview
- object portrait or staged scene for one memorable proof object
- instrument or spatial viewport for legitimate inspection and control
- editorial spread or reference desk for authority and curated evidence
- gallery or collection path for discovery across distinct objects
- queue, lane, or calendar system for constrained resources and throughput

The family determines relationships, not decoration. If several directions are requested, assign each a different family before changing palette or copy. If the user combines directions, state which contract fields come from each and discard the rest instead of blending every idea.

## Experience Architecture

Composition family describes relationships inside one region. Experience architecture describes how content beats, region geometry, interaction states, media, and handoffs form a complete route or site. Resolve both for any substantial brand, landing, SaaS, portfolio, commerce, or experimental website.

Keep these units independent:

- `content beat`: the meaning, argument, proof, collection item, action, or emotional change
- `region`: the layout and interaction container that carries one or more beats
- `viewport`: the current visible window, not a design unit
- `state`: a meaningful condition within a region before, during, or after input
- `media set`: any combination of stills, sequences, video, product UI, SVG, canvas/WebGL/3D, typography, texture, or audio

A beat may be shorter than, equal to, or longer than a viewport. One pinned region may contain several states; several ordinary-flow beats may share one visual region; one asset may persist across several beats. Full-viewport scenes, sticky chapters, snap segments, horizontal tracks, spatial canvases, and ordinary document flow are optional geometries, not definitions of `hero` or `page`.

Record the architecture before styling individual regions:

```text
Region geometry record
- content beats served:
- height mode: intrinsic | subviewport | viewport | overviewport/pinned | horizontal track | spatial canvas
- pin range or internal travel:
- interaction states and thresholds:
- media set:
- entry, handoff, and exit:
- proof, conversion, or orientation role:
- target devices and input model:
```

Possible architectures include scene-to-scene cinematic progression, persistent-object transformation, editorial chapter flow, product walkthrough, interactive collection path, mechanism-to-result reveal, proof progression, and spatial exploration. These are reasoning patterns, not presets. A route may change composition family and effect mechanism by region when the content changes, while material, type, motion grammar, or another continuity rule keeps the whole experience recognizable.

A direction for a complete route or site is incomplete if it shows only the first viewport. A full-page still can suggest section rhythm, but sticky, snap, scroll-scrub, drag, spatial travel, interruption, and state handoffs require temporal or runtime evidence.

## Direction Orthogonality

A valid direction set contains internally coherent options with visibly different spatial theses: each option has its own silhouette, way of placing control, proof object, material behavior, and density rhythm while remaining credible for the same brief.

Compare each candidate with every accepted direction across these variables:

| Comparison variable | Question |
| --- | --- |
| background material | Does the world feel physically or digitally different? |
| dominant silhouette | Is the thumbnail contour materially different? |
| primary layout grammar | Are objects related through a different spatial system? |
| navigation/control placement | Does agency enter from a different place? |
| proof object | Is a different form of evidence carrying the claim? |
| accent palette | Do accent roles and temperature create a different tension? |
| density rhythm | Does information expand and contract differently? |
| experience architecture | Do content beats, region geometries, interaction states, and handoffs form a materially different narrative and temporal system? |
| icon/decoration language | Are detail marks generated by a different system? |

Direction orthogonality requires at least four variables to differ. For a complete website direction, one of those differences must be experience architecture or page rhythm. If a candidate shares five or more variables with an accepted direction, reject it as same-looking and move to an opposing composition family or experience architecture.

Treat these repeated patterns as convergence warnings:

- warm ivory background + central horizontal rail or timeline + right action panel + rounded cards
- large centered object + supporting cards orbiting it
- left list + center document or work surface + right recommendation panel
- magnifier, lens, or inspection object reused across unrelated families
- paper-like UI + fine-border cards + green/red status chips used as the universal tasteful repair

Do not repair convergence by making the same structure merely warmer, quieter, or more saturated. Change the spatial metaphor.

## Typography And Palette Judgment

Give typography explicit roles: display or title, body, data, control, and metadata. Start with fonts available in the product or brand context. Use scale, weight, line length, and spacing to create hierarchy before adding containers; keep localized labels and dense values legible.

Derive the palette from supplied brand assets, product state, subject material, environment, or an explicit emotional aim. Assign base, surface, text, semantic, emotional-accent, and action roles. Keep accents rare enough to carry agency or memory, distinguish status with more than color, and preserve readable contrast. Minimalism may be colorful; black-and-white, cream, neon, or a gradient is never self-justifying.

## Generic-Default Test

Run a generic-default test before accepting the contract. Copy only relevant rows into `anti-defaults`, and preserve all three columns so a rejection can be reversed by evidence rather than taste impulse.

| Anti-default | Task context that makes it weak | Required override evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Warm-cream editorial field | Operational, technical, or data-heavy work where paper mood hides state and throughput | A supplied reference, material source, or brand system that makes editorial warmth native while task states remain clear |
| Near-black surface with neon accent | Ordinary SaaS or professional work where darkness implies false danger or spectacle | A real viewing environment, instrument domain, or established brand palette that requires dark adaptation |
| Broadsheet rules and dense columns | Non-editorial products whose content lacks long-form hierarchy | Real editorial content, comparison behavior, or a reference whose column structure carries the user job |
| Centered hero, abstract object, three equal cards | Brand pages that must prove a specific workflow or offer | A supplied composition or real product object that makes the center stage the clearest proof and gives each card a distinct job |
| Neutral type with no role contrast | Any surface where hierarchy, data, or action cannot be scanned quickly | An established type system whose weight, spacing, and layout create demonstrable role separation |
| Numbered section markers | Pages with no real order, progress, or narrative dependency | Content with an explicit sequence and markers that improve orientation or continuation |
| Decorative badges, pills, or pseudo-system labels | Products whose state model does not use those objects | Real categories, statuses, permissions, or controls represented by the marks |
| Dashboard collage | Work surfaces that require one primary task or brand pages that require one credible proof | Multiple simultaneous views are essential to a real decision and share a coherent data and interaction model |
| Purple-blue gradient, glow, glass, or floating orb identity | Products with no semantic or material reason for synthetic depth | A source-derived brand system, product state, or interaction behavior that gives each effect a specific role |

## Override Conditions

Write an Override condition as a concrete evidence predicate and name the contract fields it changes. Valid evidence includes a supplied reference, an established brand or repository system, verified content and assets, task or data semantics, physical viewing conditions, platform and accessibility constraints, or an explicit user decision. Novelty, personal habit, and a vague claim that a pattern looks premium are not evidence.

When evidence overrides an anti-default, retain the evidence beside the decision. When new evidence changes the central thesis, revise the contract explicitly instead of allowing implementation details to drift.

## Quality Score

Score the selected direction from 1 to 5 on Fit, Form, Proportion, Specificity, Intentional control, Tension, Craft, and Memory:

- `1`: contradictory, accidental, or template-like
- `3`: coherent and usable but still ordinary
- `5`: evidence-backed, resolved, product-specific, and memorable

Name the evidence for each score. Evaluate simultaneous complexity separately from sequential richness: a layered, highly animated route can remain controlled when each moment has clear hierarchy, pacing, transformation rules, and an exit. If Form, Proportion, or Memory is `2` or lower, redesign the composition before polishing type, color, or detail. A high average cannot hide a critical weak dimension.

## Repair Selection

Choose the lowest credible score as the single repair axis:

- weak Fit: restate the user mission and choose a better family
- weak Form, Proportion, or Memory: replace the composition family or dominant silhouette
- weak Specificity: replace decoration with a product-native object, state, or material
- weak Intentional control: reduce simultaneous competition, clarify timing and handoffs, or apply the Deletion rule without flattening the whole sequence
- weak Tension: clarify the stance, palette roles, or type-role contrast
- weak Craft: resolve typography, palette, material, and state details without changing the thesis

Patch one slice, then re-score the same dimensions. Preserve successful fields so repair does not become an uncontrolled redesign.

## Common Failures

- Treating the direction as a list of fashionable adjectives.
- Varying only color, copy, or card styling while reusing one component tree.
- Showing every valid idea at once instead of establishing a dominant form.
- Accepting a safe, neutral result with no product-specific memory.
- Using premium beige, dark glow, cockpit chrome, or cultural motifs without source evidence.
- Letting labels compensate for weak grouping, state, or silhouette.
- Turning the three parameters into defaults or routing decisions.
- Writing bare anti-defaults with no task context or override evidence.
- Polishing a low-scoring composition instead of changing its structural family.
- Presenting one polished first viewport as a complete route or site.
- Equating a content beat with a viewport, section, asset, or effect.
- Repeating one composition or entrance preset with new crops or copy instead of building meaningful state progression.
- Treating low effect count as taste, or adding many simultaneous effects without hierarchy and input control.
